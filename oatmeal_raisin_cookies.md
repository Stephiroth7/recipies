## OATMEAL RAISIN COOKIES (SOFT & CHEWY)
from Sally's Baking Addiction

**INGREDIENTS** 

* 1 cup (226g) unsalted room temperature butter
* 1 cup (100g) packed light or dark brown sugar
* 1/4 cup (50g) white granulated sugar
* 2 large room temperature eggs
* 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
* 1 tablespoon (15mL) unsulphured or dark molasses (not blackstrap; try Grandma's brand)
* 1 2/3 cups (209g) all purpose flour
* 1 teaspoon baking soda
* 1 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon (up t 2 tsp, to taste)
* 1/2 teaspoon salt (up to 1 tsp, to taste)
* 3 cups (225g) old fashioned rolled oats
* 1 cup (140g) raisins, soaked in warm water 10 minutes, drained, and patted dry
* 1/2 cup optional mix-ins: chopped toasted nuts (walnuts/pecans, etc.)

Makes app'x 1000g of dough: 16 XL cookies (3 tbsp/60g) | 20 medium cookies (2 tbsp/50g)


**PREPARATION**

1. Preheat oven to 350F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a small bowl, mix the dry ingredients together: flour, baking soda, salt and cinnamon, so it's completely homogenous. Set aside
3. In a medium bowl, use a hand mixer to cream together the wet ingredients: softened butter and both sugars until smooth. (about 3 mins)
4. Add the eggs to the wet ingredient mixture and beat on high until combined. Scrape down the sides of the bowl.
5. Add the molasses and vanilla to the wet ingredients and beat on high until combined.
6. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix on low until combined, or use a spatula to combine.
7. Fold in the oatmeal, raisins, and other mix-ins into the dough until combined. The dough will be thick and very sticky.
8. Cover, and chill the dough for 30 mins to 1 hour.
9. Portion the dough into about 2 tbsp balls and place 2 inches apart on the baking sheet.
10. Bake 12 - 14 mins until very lightlyt browned on the sides. Remove from oven and let cool on baking sheet for 5 mins before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

#### NOTES

* convection baking: 305 super convection for 10 - 11 mins.
* unbaked cookie dough can be stored in fridge for up to 3 months; bake from frozen for an extra minute.
