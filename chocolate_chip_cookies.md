## CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIES (CHEWY)
from Sally's Baking Addiction

**INGREDIENTS** 

* 2 1/4 (281g) cups flour
* 1 teaspoon baking soda
* 1 1/2 tsp cornstarch
* 3⁄4 - 1 teaspoon salt (to taste)
* 2 room-temperature eggs: 1 whole egg, 1 egg yolk whisked together.
* 1/2 cup (100g) white sugar
* 3/4 cup (150g) packed brown sugar
* 3/4 cup (170g) butter, melted and cooled for 5 minutes
* 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
* 1 1/4 cups (225g) mix-ins: semi-sweet chips or chocolate chunks or toasted nuts, etc.

Makes app'x 1000g of dough:  16 XL cookies (3 tbsp/60g) | 20 medium cookies (2 tbsp/50g)


**PREPARATION**

1. Preheat oven to 325. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. Mix the flour, cornstarch, baking soda, and salt together so it's completely homogenous.
3. In a medium bowl, whisk together the sugar and melted butter until there are no lumps.
4. In the same bowl, add the egg mixture and vanilla. The mixture will be thin.
5. Add the flour mixture to the liquid mixture and stir until combined. The dough will be soft, thick, and shiny.
6. Fold the mix-ins into the dough until combined. (The chocolate may fight this due to the butter content.)
7. Cover the dough and refrigerate for at least two hours (overnight is better). Chilled dough will prevent overspreading when baking.  
8. Portion the cookies, roll into a ball so it's more cylinder than sphere
9. Bake cookies for 13-14 mins until edges are very lightly browned but centers are soft. Cool on the pan for 10 mins before transferring to a rack.

#### NOTES

* convection baking: 305 super convection for 10 - 11 mins.
* cookie dough can be made ahead and stored in fridge for up to 3 days.
* unbaked cookie dough can be stored in fridge for up to 3 months; bake from frozen for an extra minute.
