# Oatmeal Raisin Cookies
from Sally's Cookie Addiction

* Makes app'x. 36 Cookies
* Requires chilling dough min 2 hours

**INGREDIENTS**
* 1 ½ (180 g) cups all purpose flour
* 1 teaspoon salt
* 1 teaspoon baking soda
* 1 ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
* 1 cup (2 sticks or 240 g) unsalted butter, softened
* 1 cup (225 g) packed brown sugar
* ½ cup (100 g) granulated white sugar
* 2 large eggs, room temperature
* 1 tablespoon (20 g) dark or unsulphured molasses
* 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
* 3 cups (270 g) old fashioned whole rolled oats
* 1 ½ cups (220 g) raisins, soaked in hot water for 30 mins
* ½ cup (60 g) chopped toasted nuts (pecans, walnuts)


**PREPARATION**

Pat dry the raisins that have been soaking
Preheat oven to 350F (310 super convection) 
Cover half sheet baking pan in parchment paper 

* Whisk flour, baking soda, cinnamon and salt together and set aside.
* In a large bowl, use a hand mixer to cream the butter until smooth, about 1 minute.
* Add the brown and white sugars to the butter and cream together until mixed well, about 2 minutes.
* Add the eggs, vanilla, and molasses. Scrape the sides of the bowl and continue beating to make sure it's all combined.
* Add the previously mixed dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, and combine on low speed. 
* Add the oats, raisins, and walnuts while mixing on low. (Alternatively, switch to hand mixing via spatula.)
* Cover, and chill the dough for at least 1 hour and up to 4 days. If chilling longer than 3 hours, let the dough come to room temp for about 30 minutes before using.
* Scoop approximately 2 tablespoons of dough per cookie and place 2 inches apart on a lined baking sheet
  *  or slightly smaller 1.5 tbsp cookies, a purple #40 scoop
* Bake 12 - 13 minutes until lightly browned on the sides - the centers will look soft.
* Cool on a wire rack for 5 minutes after removing from oven

NOTES:
* Freeze the individual cookie balls and bake directly from frozen at 310 super convection for 13 - 15 minutes.


STORAGE:
* Unbaked, unassembled cookies can be stored in the freezer in an airtight container.
* Baked & cooled cookies can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week.
