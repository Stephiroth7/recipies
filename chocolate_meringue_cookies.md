## CHOCOLATE MERINGUE COOKIES

(makes 20 huge cookies or 45ish small ones)

**INGREDIENTS**

* 1 ½ - 2 cups bittersweet chocolate chips (about 9 - 12 ounces or 300 - 340 grams), divided into half.  
* 4 large egg whites, room temperature  
* 1 ½ cups (powdered or granulated) sugar, divided in thirds
* ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
* ½ tsp vanilla extract
* ½ tsp white or cider vinegar (optional - this additional acid is also for stabilization)
* 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional - this is for stabilization)
* ½ teaspoon salt
* 1 - 1 ½ cup finely chopped nuts (optional)

**PREPARATION**

Preheat oven to 400°F. 
Line 2 large baking sheets with parchment paper.

* Gently melt 1 cup of chocolate chips in a bowl in microwave, stirring until no chunks remain, about 2 minutes.
* Add the vanilla extract to the melted chocolate and let cool to lukewarm.
* In a separate bowl, mix together the sugar, cocoa, and cornstarch.
* Using an electric mixer, beat the egg whites on high in large bowl until foamy.
* Add salt to the egg mixture and continue beating until shiny.
* Gradually beat in the sugar, cocoa, and optional cornstarch, adding it *slowly*. 
* Continue beating until mixture resembles soft marshmallow creme. Set aside the mixer and get a spatula.
* By hand, fold in the lukewarm melted chocolate and optional nuts + solid chocolate chips into the egg-sugar mixture.
* Place 1 or 1½ tablespoonfuls (pink or purple scoop) of the chocolate meringue on prepared baking sheet, spacing 2 inches apart. 
* Bake for 10 minutes or until puffed. 

#### NOTES: 
* The chocolate chips do not need to be divided unless you want the extra bulk. All of the chocolate can be melted.
* Keep an eye on the meringues; when they're overbaked they get hard once cooled. They should NOT have darker-colored edges.
* Cool on sheets for 5 minutes and transfer to rack to cool further.  These get super puffy!
