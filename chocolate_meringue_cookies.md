## CHOCOLATE MERINGUE COOKIES

(makes 20 huge cookies or 45ish small ones)

**INGREDIENTS**

* 1 1/2 - 2 cups bittersweet chocolate chips (about 9 - 12 ounces or 300 - 340 grams), divided into half.  
* 4 large egg whites, room temperature  
* 1 1/2 cups (powdered or granulated) sugar, divided in thirds
* 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
* 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
* 1/2 tsp white or cider vinegar (optional - this additional acid is also for stabilization)
* 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional - this is for stabilization)
* 1/2 teaspoon salt
* 1 - 1 1/2 cup finely chopped nuts (optional)

**PREPARATION**

1. Preheat oven to 400°F. 
2. Grease or line 2 large baking sheets with parchment paper.
3. Gently melt 1 cup of chocolate chips in a bowl in microwave, stirring until no chunks remain, about 2 minutes. Add vanilla to chocolate and let cool slightly.
5. In a separate bowl, mix 1/3 of the sugar, cocoa, cornstarch.
6. Using electric mixer, beat the egg whites in large bowl to foamy. Add salt and continue beating until shiny.
7. Gradually beat in remaining 1/3 of the sugar, cocoa, and optional cornstarch, adding it *slowly*. 
8. Continue beating until mixture resembles soft marshmallow creme. Set aside the mixer and get a spatula.
10. Fold in the lukewarm melted chocolate and optional nuts + solid chocolate chips.
11. Place 1 tablespoonfuls (pink scoop) of chocolate meringue on prepared sheet, spacing 2 inches apart. 
12. Bake for 10 minutes or until puffed. 

#### NOTES: 
* The chocolate chips do not need to be divided unless you want the extra bulk. All of the chocolate can be melted.
* Keep an eye on the meringues; when they're overbaked they get hard once cooled. They should NOT have darker-colored edges.
* Cool on sheets for 5 minutes and transfer to rack to cool further.  These get super puffy!
