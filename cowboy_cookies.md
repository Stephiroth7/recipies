# Cowboy Cookies

* Makes app'x. 36 small cookies
* Requires chilling dough

**INGREDIENTS**

* 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
* 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
* 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
* 1 ½ teaspoons cinnamon
* ½ teaspoon salt

* 1 ½ sticks (170 g, 12 tbsp, ¾ cup) room temperature butter
* ¾ cup white granulated sugar
* ¾ cup dark brown sugar
* 1 ½ large eggs, beaten (either 1 whole egg or 2 eggs minus a tablespoon - this is half of the original 3 eggs)
* 1 ½ teaspoons vanilla extract

* 1 ½ cups semisweet chocolate chips
* 1 ½ cups old-fashioned rolled oats
* 1 cup sweetened flaked coconut (we used shredded)
* 1 cup chopped toasted pecans



**PREPARATION**

Makes app'x. 36 cookies of 1.5 tbsp or orchid scoop size.
Preheat oven to 350°F
Cover half sheet baking pans in parchment paper 

* Mix together the dry ingredients until well combined.
* In a medium mixing bowl, cream the butter at medium speed with a hand mixer.
* Add the white sugar and brown sugar to the butter, and beat to combine, about 2 more minutes.
* Add the eggs one half at a time while beating.
* Add the vanilla extract while beating.
* Reduce the mixing speed to low and add the dry ingredients one half at a time until barely incorporated.
* Switch to mixing by hand, add the mix-ins (chocolate chips, oats, coconut, toasted pecans) to the dough.
* Cover, and chill the dough for minimum 1 hour or up to overnight.

* Scoop ¼ cup balls for large cookies, or 1.5 purple scoop for small cookies.
* Bake at 350°F for 14 to 18 minutes for large cookies, or 13 to 16 minutes for small cookies.
* Rotate the pan halfway through baking for even cooking.
* Remove from oven and let cool on the baking sheets for 5 minutes. Then remove the cookies to a rack to cool completely.

NOTES:
* Freeze the individual cookie balls up to 3 months and bake directly from frozen at 300 super convection for 13 - 15 minutes.


STORAGE:
* Unbaked, unassembled cookies can be stored in the freezer in an airtight container.
* Baked & cooled cookies can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week.
